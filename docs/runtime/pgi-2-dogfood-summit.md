schemas, or create an external platform. It produced local governance objects,
validators, fixtures, tests, and receipts.

## Review

PGI-2 is locally closed. It gives Ordivon a practical operating system for:

- deciding what evidence is needed before action
- slowing down high side-effect or irreversible work
- separating controllable process from external outcomes
- treating fatigue and emotion as governance inputs without surveillance
- blocking finance FOMO and broker-write drift
- requiring learning to become output or application
- making Ordivon construction review its own complexity

## PGI-3 Readiness Constraints

PGI-3 may start only under these boundaries:

- self-model entries must distinguish pattern from verdict
- review-to-rule extraction must keep CandidateRule advisory
- memory/content hygiene must not treat stale notes as current truth
- AI onboarding must not turn philosophy into authority overreach
- externalization must preserve companion-governance root
- no public standard, platform, broker write, action authorization, or Policy activation is opened

## Rule Update

CandidateRule proposals from PGI-2 remain candidate-only:

```text
PGI-CR-011 high side-effect actions require review and known reversibility
PGI-CR-012 reviews must not infer process quality solely from outcomes
PGI-CR-013 stalled work should classify constraints before escalating effort
PGI-CR-014 exhausted/ill/high-fatigue states block high-consequence decisions
PGI-CR-015 block-level FOMO/self-proof risk requires hold/reject posture
PGI-CR-016 major learning blocks require output/application loop
PGI-CR-017 complexity-increasing Ordivon changes require debt and evidence
PGI-CR-018 relationship/emotion reviews record patterns, not intimate raw data
```

None are Policy.
