## Purpose

Philosophy is not decorative knowledge for Ordivon. It is the highest
governance layer: the layer that decides how truth is judged, how values are
chosen, how action is constrained, how pain is interpreted, and how the self
evolves under uncertainty.

The root question is:

```text
How can a finite person judge reality, choose values, constrain action,
bear uncertainty and pain, form a self, and continue evolving?
```

Ordivon should not begin philosophical learning as history memorization. It
should begin from this first-principles question and turn philosophical insight
into governance objects.

## Non-Goals

This layer is not:

- a philosophy encyclopedia
- mysticism
- a substitute for medical, legal, or financial advice
- a justification for over-recording life
- a policy activation mechanism
- an authority override for evidence, review, or explicit NO-GO boundaries

Philosophy gives Ordivon higher-order orientation. It does not bypass receipts,
tests, reviews, gates, or human judgment.

## Five-Stage Learning Path

### Stage 1 - Judge Reality

Study epistemology, informal logic, evidence, scientific method, falsifiability,
and Bayesian updating.

Goal:

```text
Do not be carried away by hallucination, market narrative, self-comfort, or
unverified AI output.
```

### Stage 2 - Judge Value

Study consequentialism, deontology, virtue ethics, Aristotle, Kant, Stoicism,
and Confucian self-cultivation.

Goal:

```text
Know what is worth pursuing, what cannot be done, and what kind of person the
system should help form.
```

### Stage 3 - Judge Action

Study practical reason, decision theory, bounded rationality, behavioral
economics, opportunity cost, reversibility, and implementation intentions.

Goal:

```text
Turn desire into decision gates, constraints, receipts, and small reversible
experiments.
```

### Stage 4 - Bear Pain and Uncertainty

Study Stoicism, existentialism, Buddhism, Daoism, and psychological flexibility.

Goal:

```text
Do not collapse under failure, inflate under success, or lose yourself in
comparison.
```

### Stage 5 - Externalize Into System

Study pragmatism, systems thinking, extended mind, AI governance, and Ordivon's
own dogfood records.

Goal:

```text
Turn philosophy into action, feedback, tools, documents, AI collaboration, and
governance flywheels.
```

## Decision Gate Implications

High-consequence decisions should carry these fields before action:

| Field | Why it exists |
|-------|---------------|
| claim | Logic: what is being asserted? |
| evidence | Epistemology: why believe it? |
| confidence | Bayesian: how strong is belief? |
| base_rate | Bayesian: what usually happens? |
| downside | Decision theory: what can go wrong? |
| reversibility | Practical reason: can this be undone? |
| no_go_check | Deontology: does it violate principle? |
| character_effect | Virtue ethics: what kind of person does this train? |
| controllable_boundary | Stoicism: what is mine to govern? |
| attachment_check | Buddhism / Daoism: is craving or over-force driving it? |
| next_review | Pragmatism: when does experience update the rule? |

## Anti-Overforce Rule

If the system detects repeated effort without movement, the default response is
not "try harder." The first review question is:

```text
Is the constraint physical, emotional, strategic, environmental, or conceptual?
```

This protects Ordivon from turning ambition into compulsion. Body, sleep,
emotion, context, and system design are legitimate governance inputs.
